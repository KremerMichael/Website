Hello there! My name is

Michael Kremer.

I love Electricity.

I'm a senior studying Electrical Engineering at Boston University. Take a look at some of my work below!

Who am I?

Raised on Legos and Mythbusters, I've always had a deep love for engineering. My love for electricity developed later in life after building a PC so I could play video games and assembling a home DAC/AMP kit so I could listen to high-fidelity music. After getting hooked on electricity, I've pursued this love full force.

Currently I'm working at the Boston University Electronic Design Facility as a Student Technicians. As a true contract engineering chop-shop, the EDF has exposed me to an incredible range of technologies. All at once I may be writing firmware for CERN, Designing and debugging Multi-Layer PCBS, and running a soldering/software workshop for Women in STEM.

Outside of school and work, I try my hardest to be an active member of the Psi Delta Chapter of Theta Tau serving as the Brotherhood, Academic, Public Relations, and Proffessional Development chairs. When I get free-time outside of all this, I love to read, find new restraunts with my friends, and go on runs.
